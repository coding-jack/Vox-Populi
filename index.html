Consecutive Interpretation

Consecutive Interpretation is a mode of interpretation where the speaker pauses while speaking to allow the interpreter to convey the message into the target languages. Among many other settings, this type of interpretation is generally used for smaller gatherings, depositions, immigration proceedings, medical appointments, facility tours, business meetings, trainings, and interviews.

Simultaneous Interpretation

In the world today, simultaneous interpretation is classified as the most demanding and most cost effect. It is used in Government settings, and the interpreter has to interpret what the speaker wants to say within the time allowed by the speaker’s pace, without changing the natural flow of speech. It is the job of the interpreter to deliver all semantic elements in the speech including its tone. The interpreter also has to deliver the intent of the message the speaker wants to convey. This type of interpretation requires a super talented professional that only practices this interpreting mode, and Vox Populi enforces this qualification.

Sight Interpretation

A sight translation is an oral translation of a written text. It is as difficult as simultaneous interpretation, and involves some of the same mental processes. Our interpreters are skillful in pacing the time between reading the written text while translating mentally and rendering orally the translated version of of the written original text, in a time manner without distracting the listener. This type of interpretation is often used in Medical, Legal, and Business fields.
